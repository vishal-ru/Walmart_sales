##  Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing Walmart retail sales data to uncover trends, seasonality, store performance, and the impact of holidays on sales. The goal is to generate actionable insights that support data-driven decision-making in inventory planning and sales forecasting.

---

##  Objectives
- Analyze weekly sales trends across stores
- Identify seasonal and monthly patterns
- Compare holiday vs non-holiday sales performance
- Rank top and bottom performing stores
- Study correlation between sales and economic indicators

---

##  Dataset
- Source: Walmart Sales Dataset
- Records: 643,000+ weekly sales records
- Features:
  - Store
  - Date
  - Weekly_Sales
  - Holiday_Flag
  - Temperature
  - Fuel_Price
  - CPI
  - Unemployment

---

##  Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas, NumPy
- Matplotlib, Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

##  Key Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning & preprocessing
- Feature engineering (Year, Month, Week, Quarter)
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Holiday vs Non-Holiday sales comparison
- Store-level performance analysis
- Correlation analysis with economic indicators

---

##  Key Insights
- Holiday weeks show **18â€“25% higher average sales** compared to non-holiday weeks.
- Sales demonstrate strong seasonality, peaking during festive months.
- A small group of stores consistently outperform others in average weekly sales.
- Economic indicators show weak-to-moderate correlation with short-term sales.

---

##  Conclusion
The analysis highlights that **seasonality and holidays are the primary drivers of sales**, outweighing short-term economic fluctuations. These insights can help optimize promotional strategies, inventory allocation, and demand forecasting.
